Maintaining a toaster computer: the HP ProBook 4520s

This is my beloved toaster. A machine I found in the garbage a few years back. It was a sickly thing, with only 2GB of DDR3, 300GB of spinning rust, and Windows 7 Home 32-bit. Even though this was a 64-bit machine. It was incredibly slow to start up, committed 80% of its memory to bloatware and Windows, and promptly grinded to a halt the moment more than 4 browser tabs were open. It was a mediorce computer back in 2011 when it launched, and when I found it in 2016, it was absolutely unusable.

And that was only the start.
